4.3 Projectile Motion:

-What is projectile motion? Motion of an object projected into the air, subjected only to gravitational force.

-What are some examples of projectile motion? Fireworks, motion of any ball in sports.

-What is projectile and trajectory? An object in projectile motion. The path of projectile.

-Why can the motions along perpendicular axes be analyzed independently? because they are independent

-What is the key to analyzing two-dimensional projectile motion? Break down into two motion: along horizontal and vertical axis.

-What must be included to describe projectile motion completely? Velocity, acceleration and displacement.

-Why the initial velocity in X-direction is equal to final velocity in X-direction? The gravity is vertical and acceleration along X- direction is zero.

-What is the magnitude of the horizontal component of acceleration? Zero.

-There are two sets of kinematic equations used for projectile motions. The one along horizontal (X-axis) direction has acceleration zero while along verticle direction (Y-axis) has acceleration is "-g". Look at the set of equations to analyze the projectile motion. (Equation 4.19 to 4.23)

-Problem-solving strategy: Seperate the varibales into two components (X- and Y-). Solve them with two different sets of kinematic equations. Recombine the components at last. (Same as that of motion in 2D in earlier section. Keep in mind of the values of acceleration in two components. Also, keep in mind that the Y-component of the velocity at the heighest level is zero.)
