5.1 Forces

- What is kinematics? What is Dynamics? Kinematics – study of motion. Dynamics – study of how forces affect the motion of objects.

- Who is Newtonian Mechanics named for? Isaac Newton

- What is force? Is it a scalar or vector? A push or pull. A vector.

- What is a free-body diagram? What forces are drawn on it? Sketch showing all external forces action on an object. Only external forces.

- What is the problem-solving strategy for drawing free-body diagrams? Draw the object. Include all forces that act on the object, representing these forces as vectors. Resolve all force vectors into x- and y-components. Draw a separate free-body diagram for each object in the problem.

- What is a restoring force? Force exerted on an object to return it to its relaxed state.

- What are contact forces? Field forces? Contact forces are caused byphysical contact between objects.

- What is a newton? What combination of base units is the Newton equivalent to? SI unit of force. kg*m/s^2.

- The resultant of two forces causes a mass to ______________. Accelerate.

- What is net external force? Vector sum of all external forces acting on a system. Equation 5.1.

5.2 Newton’s First Law

- What is Newton’s first law?A body at rest remains at rest, or, if in motion, remains in motion at a constant velocity unless acted on by a net external force.

- Is friction an external force? Yes.

- What is mass? Gravitation? Mass is a measure of the amount of something. Gravitation is the attraction of two masses.

- What is inertia? What is the law of inertia known as? Inertia is the ability of an object to resist a change in motion. The law of intertia is also known as Newton’s first law.

- What is an inertial reference frame? A reference frame moving at constant velocity relative to an inertial frame is also inertial. A reference frame accelerating relative to an inertial frame is not inertial.

- What does it mean for a system to be in equilibrium? The forces on a system are balanced.

- What does Equation 5.2 say? A net force of zero implies that the velocity of the object is constant.

- If a car is moving at constant velocity, what is the net force acting on it? See Figure 5.9. Zero.

5.4 Mass and Weight

- What is weight? Force due to gravity in a mass.

- Is weight the same thing as mass? How are they related? No. Mass doesn’t change, weight is on object’s mass times its acceleration.

- If you were on another planet, would your weight or mass change? Neither? Both? Mass stays the same because it is intrinsic to the body. The weight changes because the acceleration due to gravity changes.

- What does it mean for an object to be in free fall? The net external force equals its’ weight.

- Mass is an _____________ property of matter. Intrinsic.

- What is a normal force? The force supporting the weight of an object, or a load, that is perpendicular to the surface of contact between the load and its support; the surface applies this force to an object to support the weight of the object.

- What is the vector form of the normal force of an object resting on a horizontal surface? Its magnitude? Equation 5.11 and 5.12.

5.7 Drawing Free-Body Diagrams

- What is the problem-solving strategy for drawing free-body diagrams? Draw the object. At first, you may want to draw a circle around the object of interest to be sure you focus on labeling the forces acting on the object. We often place this point at the origin of an xy-coordinate system. Include all forces that act on the object, representing these forces as vectors. Consider the types of forces (normal force, friction, tension, and spring force, weight applied force). Do not include the net force on the object. Convert the free-body diagram into a more detailed diagram showing the x- and y-components of a given force. If there are two or more objects, in the problem draw a separate free-body diagram for each object.
