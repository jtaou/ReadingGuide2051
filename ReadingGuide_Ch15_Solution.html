15.1 – Simple Harmonic Motion

- What is periodic motion? Any motion that repeats itself at regular intervals.

- What is period? frequency? How do they relate? Period - time it takes to do 1 oscillation. Frequency - number of oscillations per unit time. Equation 15.1.

- What is the SI unit of frequency? What other base units is it equivalent to? Hertz. 1Hz = 1/s.

- What is simple harmonic motion? The net force of the system is proportional to the displacement and acts in the opposite direction of the displacement.

- What is amplitude? Maximum displacement from equilibrium.

- What is equilibrium position? What is the net force at the equilibrium position? Position where a spring is neither stretched nor compressed. The net force is zero.

- What is the equation for the position as a function of time for simple harmonic motion? See Figure 15.4 and 15.5 to see how to apply it. Equation 15.2.

- What is a phase shift? Angle, in radians, that is used in a cosine or sine function to shift the function left or right, used to match up the function with the initial conditions of the data.

- Understand how to obtain the velocity and acceleration vs time for from the position function.

- Know equations 15.3-15.8. Example 15.2 (Determining the Equations of Motion for a Block and a Spring) shows how to use them.

- What are the equations for angular frequency, period, and frequency for a mass on a spring? Equations 15.9, 15.10, 15.11.

- Understand Figure 15.10. It helps visualize the position, velocity, and acceleration of a mass hanging from a spring.

15.4 – Pendulums

- What is a simple pendulum? A point mas (pendulum bob) suspended from a string with negligible mass.

- What are the equations for angular frequency and period for simple pendulum? Equations 15.17, 15.18, 15.19.

- Check the small angle approximation using your calculator. For small angles (<15 degrees), sin theta ~ theta, when theta is measured in radians.

- Example 15.2 (Measuring Acceleration due to Gravity by the Period of a Pendulum) shows you can use a pendulum to measure g.

- What is a physical pendulum? How is it different from a simple pendulum? Any object that oscillates like a simple pendulum, but cannot be modelled as a point mass on a string.

- What are the equations for angular frequency and period for a physical pendulum? Equations 15.20, 15.21.

- What is torsional pendulum? Rigid body suspended a light wire or spring. When it is twisted, the body oscillates.

- What is the equation for period for a torsional pendulum? What is kappa? What are its units? Equation 15.22. Kappa is a torsion constant, has units of kg*m^2/s^2.
