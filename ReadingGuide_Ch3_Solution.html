3.1 Position, Displacement, and Average Velocity:

Position:

  • What is position?
    • where object is at any particular time.
  • What is frame of reference?
    • Arbitrary set of axes from which the position and motion of an object are described.
  • What frame of references are used to describe the motion of objects on earth and position of a person in an airplane?
    • Earth. Plane

Displacement:

  • What is displacement?
    • change in positon
  • Is displacement a vector or scalar?
    • vector
  • How is displacement related to initial and final position?
    • Disp = Final - Initial position
  • What does the upper case Greek letter Delta Δ mean here?
    • means "change in" whatever quantity follows it
  • How do you find the total displacement from two more more individual displacements?
    • Sum of the individual displacement
  • How is total displacement different from total distance traveled?
    • Distance is the sum of MAGNITUDES of individual displacements instead of vector sum of disp vectors

Average Velocity:

  • How is the time variable important to describe motion?
    • allows to state the rate of change of position during motion
  • What is average velocity? Is it vector or scalar?
    • Ratio of total displacement to time taken. Vector.

3.5 Free Fall:

  • What is free fall?
    • Motion of object solely in gravitational field.
  • How does the acceleration of gravity/free fall change with the mass of objects if the air resistance is negligible?
    • it doesn't: its constant with mass, same for all objects (g)
  • How does air resistance affect the motion of object in free fall?
    • opposes it and slows it down
  • For freefall problems, what determines whether we set the acceleration a = +g or -g?
    • Determined by which direction we choose to be positive displacement/position, whether up or down. Usually we choose up as +y, so a = -g, negative, as in Eq.s 3.15-17
  • What is the average value of g on earth?
    • 9.81 m/s^2
