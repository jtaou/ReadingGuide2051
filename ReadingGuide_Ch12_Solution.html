12.1 – Conditions for Static Equilibrium

- What is equilibrium? Static equilibrium? An object is in equilibrium if both its linear and angular acceleration are zero relative to an inertial frame of reference. Static equilibrium occurs when an object is at rest in the frame of reference.

- What is the first equilibrium condition? The sum of the forces on an object must sum to zero. Equation 12.2.

- What is the first equilibrium condition? The sum of the torques acting on an object sum to zero. Equation 12.5.

- When applying equilibrium conditions to a rigid body, what should you choose as the origin (pivot point)? You are free to choose any point as the origin of the reference frame.

- What is a center of gravity? The center of mass of an object, where the weight vector should be attached.

- When does gravitational torque occur on an object? This can be seen in Figure 12.3. When the center of mass is located off the pivot point.

- In Example 12.1 (Center of Gravity of a Car), which equations are the equilibrium conditions? 1st - Equation 12.11. 2nd - Equation 12.12.

- In Example 12.2 (A Breaking Tension), notice that there are no rigid bodies to cause torque, so the 2nd equilibrium condition isn't required but the 1st condition must be applied in both the x and y directions.

12.3 – Stress, Strain, and Elastic Modulus

- What is stress? Quantity that describes the magnitude of forces that cause deformation. Generally stress per area.

- What is tensile, bulk, and shear stress?Tensile - elongation, bulk - squeezing from all sides, shear - tangential forces.

- What is a pascal? What other base Si units is it equivalent to? SI unit of force. 1 Pa = 1N/m^2.

- What is strain? Fractional change in either length of volume or geometry.

- What equation relates stress and strain? Equation 12.33.

- What stress does each modulus correspond to? Young's - tensile stress, bulk - bulk stress, shear - shear stress.

- What is the equation for Young's modulus? Equation 12.36.

- What is normal pressure? The typical atmospheric pressure of one atmosphere.

- What is the equation for bulk modulus? Equation 12.39.

- What is compressibility? The inverse of bulk modulus. Describes the change in volume of a fluid per unit increase of pressure.

- What is the equation for shear modulus? Equation 12.43.

12.4 – Elasticity and Plasticity

- What does it mean for an object to be elastic? What is the elastic limit? The object goes back to its original shape when the load isn't present. The stress limit where the object is permanently deformed.

- What is plastic behavior? The behavior of an object when it deforms irreversibly and does not return to its original shape when the load is removed.
