8.1 – Potential Energy of a System

- What is potential energy?Function of position, energy possessed by an object relative to the system considered

- In Figure 8.2, where is kinetic energy maximum? Minimum? Where is potential energy maximum? Minimum? KE: max at points 1 and 5, min at point3. PE: max at 3, min at 1 and 5.

- The idea that *difference* in potential energy is the same, no matter what the point of reference is chosen to be is extremely important both practically and philosophically. The actual value of energy at any given position is entirely arbitrary (the formal way of saying it is that energy is only determined to within an arbitrary additive constant). The difference in potential energy, however, is independent of that choice of origin. This means that you can pick the reference point for your convenience to make the problem simpler.

- How should one choose the point of zero potential energy in a problem? Choose a point that is most convenient.

8.2 – Conservative and Non-Conservative Forces

- What is a conservative force? What is a non-conservative force? Conservative - force that does work independent of path. Non-conservative – force that does work that depends on path.

- What are examples of conservative and non-conservative forces? Gravity, springs and friction, air resistance.

- The E in equation 8.9 should be an F. Work is the integral of force over the displacement.

- What is a closed path? The sum of two paths, a to b then b back to a.

- What does path independence mean for the work done by a conservative force? The path to go from a to b doesn’t matter.

8.3 – Conservation of Energy

- What is a conserved quantity? Quantity that cannot be created or destroyed, but may be transformed between different forms of itself.

- What is mechanical energy? Sum of potential and kinetic energy.

- What does the conservation of energy mean? The mechanical energy E of a particle stays constant unless forces outside the system or non-conservative forces do work on it, in which case, the change in the mechanical energy is equal to the work done by the non-conservative forces. Equation 8.12.

- What is the problem solving strategy for solving problems involving the conservation of energy? Identify the body or bodies to be studied (the system). Identify all forces acting on the body or bodies. Determine whether each force that does work is conservative. For every force that does work, choose a reference point and determine the potential energy function for the force. Apply the principle of mechanical energy conservation by setting the sum of the kinetic energies and potential energies equal at every point of interest.

8.5 – Sources of Energy

- What are other forms of energy listed in the text? Thermal energy, electrical energy, chemical energy, radiant energy, nuclear energy.

- Since energy is always conserved, why should we be concerned about our energy resources? - What is the difference between renewable and non-renewable energy sources? Renewable - energy source that is replenished by natural processes, over human time scales. Non-renewable - energy source that is not renewable, but is depleted by human consumption.
