2.1 Scalers and Vectors

-What is a scalar quantity? Quantity that is fully specified by a single number with a unit.

  • What are some examples of scalar quantities?
    • Temperature. Calories.
  • What is a vector quantity?
    • Quantity that is fully specified by a number (with units) and direction.
  • How are vectors represented geometrically/visually (see Figure 2.2)?
    • Geometrically, vectors are represented by arrows.
  • How are vectors and scalars represented in this book?
    • Vectors: boldface letter with an arrow above it. Scalars: letter without an arrow.
  • In Figure 2.3, what is the arrow that starts at point A and ends at point B called? What is its length called?
    • A displacement vector. The magnitude.
  • NOTE: The equals sign with three lines (≡) means "equivalent to"
  • Does the displacement vector tell us what the actual path of the particle is?
    • No. Just the distance and direction from the start and end points.
  • What does it mean for two vectors to be antiparallel? Parallel? Orthogonal? See the summary in Figure 2.5.
    • Antiparallel vectors point 180 degrees from each other. Parallel vectors point in the same direction. Orthogonal vectors are perpendicular to one another.

  • What is a vector equation?
    • Equation with vectors on the left and right hand sides: implies direction AND magnitude of both sides is the same
  • What is a scalar equation?
    • Equation with scalars on the left and right hand sides.
  • Understand Figure 2.7. It shows how one-dimensional vector quantities are added or subtracted.
  • If a scalar is multiplied times a vector, is the result another vector or another scalar? another vector
  • What is a resultant”? The sum of 2 or more vectors.
  • What does it mean to say that vector addition is commutative and associative?
    • Commutative – A+B=B+A. associative (A+B)+C = A+(B+C).
  • What does commutativity mean for the order in which you add vectors?
    • The order doesn’t matter.
  • NOTE: Unit vectors are an important concept we will focus on heavily in the course

    What is a unit vector? How are they represented?

    • Special vector that has magnitude = 1 and no units: specifies only a direction. A boldface letter with a ^ above it (instead of an arrow)
