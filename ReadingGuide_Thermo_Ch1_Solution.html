1.1 Temperature and Thermal Equilibrium:



-What is heat transfer?movement of energy from one to another as a result of temperature difference.

-When are the two objects in thermal equilibrium? If there is no net enrgy transferred between them even if they allow either to gain energy from other.

-What is Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics? If "A" is thermal equilibrium with "B" and "B" is thermal equilibrium with "C" then "A" and "B" are also thermal equlibrium with each other.



1.2 Thermometers and Temperature Scales:



-What can be used as the basis of a thermometer? Any physical property that depends consistently and reproducibly on temperature.

-What are the three most common temperature scales? Fahrenheit, Celsius and Kelvin.

-What is the freezing and boiling point of water in Celsius and Fehrenheit scale? Freezing: 0 oC, 32 oF . Boiling: 100 oC, 212 oF

-What is absolute zero? Lowest possible temperature at which the average kinetic energy of molecules become zero.

-What is absolute temperature scale?one whose zero point is absolute zero. Kelvin Scale.

-What is SI unit of tempreature? Kelvin (K).

1.3 Thermal Expansion:



-What is thermal expansion?change in size or volume as a result of temperature change.

-How does the increase in kinetic energy expand substance? Linnard-Jones potential describing molecular force is asymmetric. So, the distance moved by molecules away from eachother in a given kinetic energy is greater than the distance they move towards eachother.

-Is there any preferred direction of expansion? No. It is isotropic.

1.4 Heat Transfer, Specific Heat, and Calorimetry:



-What is heat? What is its unit? Type of energy transfer that is caused by temperature difference. Joule(J).

-What is internal energy? What is it proportional to? Sum of mechanical energies of constituent molecules. Temperature.

-What is mechanical equvalent of heat? What is its value? The work needed to produce the same effects as heat transfer. 1.0 Kcal = 4186 J

-What is state variable? well defined quantity that depends only on the current state of the system rather than the history of that system.

-Give example of state variables. Are heat and work state variables? Temperature, Internal Energy. No, they are not.

-What is the relation between the heat transfer and temperature change? Eqn. (1.5)

-What is specific heat capacity? amount of heat necessary to change temperature of 1.0 kg mass by 1.0 degree celcius.

-What is calorimeter? A container that prevents heat transfer in or out.

-What is calorimetry? Use of caloritmeter to make measurements.

-What is most important idea about calorimetry problem? Heat lost by hotter object is equal to heat gained by colder object.

1.5 Phase Changes:



-What does the phase of a substance depend on? Pressure and temperature.

-What is phase diagram? Plot of pressure vs. temperature showing the phase in each region.

-What is the ciritical temperataure in phase diagram of water? Temperature aboe which the liquid and gas phase can't be distinguished. Water is called supercritical fluid.

-What is tripple point in the phase diagram of water? Combination of pressure and temperature at which liquid water, ice and water vapor co-exist stably.

-What is sublimation? Phase change from solid to gas.

-When do we call gas phase a vapor? When it exists at a temperature below the boiling temperature.

-What is boiling point of a liquid at a given pressure? Temperature at which vapor pressure equals to ambient pressure.

-When does the heat transfer doesn't cause any temperature change? In a phase transition.

-Figure 1.15: Look how the temperature changes with heat over the phase change for water.

-What is heat of fusion? The energy per unit mass required to change a substance from the solid phase to the liquid phase.

-What is heat of vaporization? The energy per unit mass required to change a substance from liquid to vapor phase.
