14.1 – Fluids, Density and Pressure

- What is a fluid? Liquid or gas, yields to shearing forces.

- What is density? Mass per unit volume. Characteristic of an object. Equation 14.1.

- Common densities are listed in Table 14.1.

- What is specific gravity? Ratio of the density of a material to the density of water at 4 degrees Celsius.

- What is the SI unit of pressure? What other base units is it equivalent to? Pascal. 1 Pa = 1N/m^2.

- What equation shows how pressure changes with depth? What is p_0? Equation 14.4. The atmospheric pressure.

- Example 14.1 (What force must a Dam Withstand?) shows how to apply Equation 14.4. Notice that the force doesn't depend on the surface area of the water.

14.3 – Pascal's Principle and Hydraulics

- What is Pascal's principle? When a change is pressure is applied to an enclosed fluid, it is transmitted undimished to all portions of the fluid and the walls of its containers.

- What equation relates forces and areas in hydraulic systems? Equation 14.12.

14.4 – Archimedes' Principle and Buoyancy

- What is buoyant force? The upward force on any object in a fluid.

- What is Archimedes' principle? The buoyant force on an object equals the weight of the fluid it displaces.

- What equations relate the fraction submerged to volume displaced to the volume of an object and density of an object to density of a fluid? The equation in the middle of pdf page 715 and the equation at the top of pdf page 716.

- What is viscosity? Measure of the internal friction in a fluid.
