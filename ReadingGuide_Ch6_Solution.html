6.2 Friction

  • What is Friction?
    • Friction is a force that opposes relative motion between systems in contact.
  • What is static and kinetic friction?
    • static: two contacting surfaces are stationary relative to one another (attempted movement). Kinetic: Surfaces are moving relative to one another.
  • When pushing a crate on a concrete floor and the crate doesn’t move, are you pushing against kinetic or static friction? What friction are you pushing against once the crate starts to move?
    • Static. Kinetic.
  • Figure 6.10 shows a microscopic view of two surfaces. Friction arises in part because of the ____________ of the surfaces in contact.
    • Roughness or any word meaning similar
  • Which equation gives an expression constraining the magnitude of static friction? Why does it have a less than or equal to sign?
    • Equation 6.1. Because the expression only gives the maximum magnitude possible (μs*NormForce). It is "usually" less.

  • NOTE: "Usually" the magnitude of static friction is simply the minimum needed to counteract any other forces acting in the problem, calculated by Newton's 2nd Law considering all other forces in the problem: a "reaction" force.

    Which equation is for the magnitude of kinetic friction?
