6.2 Friction

  • What is Friction?
    • Friction is a force that opposes relative motion between systems in contact.
  • What is static and kinetic friction?
    • If two systems are in contact and stationary relative to one another, then the friction between them is called static friction. If two systems are in contact and moving relative to one another, then the friction between them is called kinetic friction.
  • When pushing a crate on a concrete floor and the crate doesn’t move, are you pushing against kinetic or static friction? What friction are you pushing against once the crate starts to move?
    • Static. Kinetic.
  • Figure 6.10 shows a microscopic view of two surfaces. Friction arises in part because of the ____________ of the surfaces in contact.
    • Roughness
  • What is the equation for the magnitude of static friction? Why does it have a less than or equal to sign?
    • Equation 6.1. Because the force acts until the object starts moving.
  • What is the equation for the magnitude of kinetic friction?
    • Equation 6.2.

    • Because of the less than or equal to sign in Equation 6.1.
  • Table 6.1 contains common coefficients of friction. Given these values, is the coefficient of static friction always larger than the coefficient of kinetic friction?
    • Yes.
  • In Example 6.10 (Static and Kinetic Friction), for each push force in (a), (b), (c), and (d), does the box move? Why or why not?
    • (a), (b), (c) – no motion, push is less than static friction force. (d) – it moves, the push overcomes the static friction force.

6.3 Centripetal Force

  • What is centripetal force? What direction does it point?
    • Any new force causing uniform circular motion. Toward the center of the circle.
  • What is the equation for centripetal force in terms of mass, velocity, angular velocity, and radius of curvature?
    • Equation 6.3.
  • When a road is banked at a _________ angle, the _________ you can take the curve.
    • Steeper. Faster.
  • What must be true for ideal banking?
    • External force equals the horizontal centripetal force in the absence of friction.
  • What equation gives the ideal banking angle?
    • Equation 6.4.
  • When you turn right in a car, you move in a __________ line, but the car moves ___________, you are not experiencing a force from the _________.
    • Straight. Right. Left.
  • The car is an _______________ because it is accelerated to the side. The forced sensed by passengers is an __________ force.
    • Noninertial frame of reference. Inertial.
  • On a merry-go-round, passengers experience an inertial force that is often referred to as a ____________force.
    • Centrifugal.
  • What scientific instrument uses the centrifugal force to hasten sedimentation?
    • Centrifuge.
  • The inertial of the particle carries sediment along what kind of path?
    • Tangent line.
  • What is the Coriolis force?
    • Inertial force causing the apparent deflection of moving objects when viewed in a rotating frame of reference
  • What is an example of a system in which the rotation of the earth must be considered?
    • Rotation of weather systems
  • Coriolis force causes hurricanes to rotate in what direction in the northern hemisphere? What about the southern hemisphere? This can be seen in Figure 6.28.
    • counterclockwise, clockwise.
  • In an inertial frame, __________explains the path and no force is found without an identifiable source.
    • Inertia.
